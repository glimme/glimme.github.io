So this story isn’t as well known, but I love it.
The ‘hero’ of this story is a little boy, who loves butter so much, he’s known only as Butterball. One day,
Butterball’s mother sees a troll-lady coming, and tells him to hide under the table. He does so, and when the
troll-lady knocks on the door and asks for him, his mother tells her that he’s out. The troll-lady says she was planning
on giving him a silver knife, and Butterball pops out from under the table, to his mother’s exasperation.
The troll-lady says the knife is in the bag she’s carrying, but her back hurts (hands up if you can see where this is going),
so could Butterball crawl into the bag to get it? He does, and she grabs the bag and hightails it out of there.
Halfway to her house, she stops to rest, and Butterball cuts the bag open with the knife that was actually in the bag.
He then puts a big root in the bag, to trick the troll-lady into thinking he’s still in there, and runs home.
The next day, the same thing happens, except with a silver fork instead of a knife.
The third day, it’s a spoon, and the troll-lady doesn’t stop until she gets home.
She tells her daughter to start making stew out of Butterball, and leaves for unknown reasons. However, her daughter is
incompetent, and doesn’t even know how to boil water. Butterball offers help, and tells her to check the temperature of
the water by sticking her head in it. She does this immediately, and he pushes her the rest of the way in.
Eventually, the troll-lady and her husband get home, and exclaim about how good the stew looks, smells and tastes, with
Butterball providing color commentary on the actual nature of the stew. Then he drops rocks on their heads, which kills
them, and goes home with silver, gold, and jewels he found somewhere.

THE END.
